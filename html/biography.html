Biography

Naganuma (Born May 16, 1972 in Hokkaidō, Japan) started his musical career at the age of five playing the electronic organ. At age fourteen he began to compose his own songs. Between the years 1993 5o 1997 Naganuma worked as a DJ and bartender while aiming to become a singer-songwriter in the J-pop industry.

When Naganuma's singer-songwriter career did not work, in 1998 he sent demo tapes of his music to Sega. His first job for Sega was voice editing for Shoujo Kakumei Utena: Itsuka Kakumei Sareru Monogatari and then first composition was for Hip Jog Jog.

In 2000, he was the lead composer for Jet Set Radio and in 2002 would also compose its sequel Jet Set Radio Future and Ollie King in 2003. In 2005, he composed most of Sonic Rush's soundtrack and was nominated at the Golden Joystick Awards for Soundtrack of the Year.

In 2006 he composed two tracks for the anime adaption of Air Gear and also remixed Fuusen Gum for the anime Gin Tama under the skankfunk alias. Hedeki was not credited at the time for his contributions to these projects due to contractual agreement with Sega. While still at Sega, Nagunama was part of Yakuza team which he was incharge of voice editing and sound effects until he left in Sega in 2008, to become a freelance composer.

Since becoming a freelance composer he has composed music for many indie games and has continued to work with Sega under the skankfunk alias on games such as Kurohyō: Ryū ga Gotoku Shinshō and Super Monkey Ball 3D.

Nagnuma is currently not active in the gaming industry and has stated that he would like to create an original album.
