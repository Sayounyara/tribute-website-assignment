Hideki Naganuma is a Japanese composer and DJ, primarily making music for video games. While Naganuma has not released any albums himself, he is planning on composing and original album. He is most known for his work in the video games Jet Set Radio and its sequel Jet Set Radio Future. In the year 1998, Naganuma sent demo tapes to Sega and was later accepted. His first works with the company were voice editing for Shoujo Kakumei Utena: Itsuka Kakumei Sareru Monogatari (Revolutionary Girl Utena: Story of the Someday Revolution), and composition for Hip Jog Jog. Naganuma took inspiration from western and big beat for the games. Other games he composed music While working for Sega were Ollie King and Sonic Rush.

During this time, Naganuma also composed music outside of Sega under the alias "skankfunk" due to contractual reasons. He composed music for an anime adaptation of Air Gear, which itself was inspired by Jet Set Radio, and for Gin Tama, but was not credited at the time.
